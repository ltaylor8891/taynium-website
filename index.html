PROBLEM

Most engineering problems live in the gaps.

Not in one big failure — in the space where machinery, CAD systems, production information and business processes have grown up separately, each solving its own problem without ever quite talking to the others.

01

A drawing gets issued from the wrong configuration, and the wrong revision reaches the shop floor.

02

A DXF goes to the laser carrying last month's profile, material or thickness data.

03

A workshop-floor modification never makes it back into the model — and the knowledge stays in someone's head.

None of this is anyone's fault. It's what happens when practical engineering knowledge lives in people, not in a controlled process. Taynium exists to close those gaps — connecting design, manufacturing, software and real-world machinery into something that actually holds together.
